What is Spider-Man?

Origin

Comic Book Origin

Creators: Spider-Man was created by writer Stan Lee and artist Steve Ditko.

First Appearance: He debuted in Amazing Fantasy #15, published in August 1962 by Marvel Comics.

Villains and Allies

Over time, Spider-Man faces iconic villains like the Green Goblin, Doctor Octopus, The Lizard, Electro, and The Vulture.

He also forms close relationships with characters like Mary Jane Watson, Gwen Stacy, and Harry Osborn.

Spider-Man in the Comics: Key Storylines

The Night Gwen Stacy Died (The Amazing Spider-Man #121, 1973): One of the most heartbreaking moments in comic history. Gwen Stacy, Peter's girlfriend, is killed by the Green Goblin.

The Clone Saga (1990s): A controversial storyline where Peter finds out he may be a clone of the real Spider-Man.

Spider-Man in TV and Animation

Spider-Man (1967): The first Spider-Man animated series that introduced the famous "Spider-Man, Spider-Man, does whatever a spider can" theme song.

Spider-Man and His Amazing Friends (1981): Spider-Man teams up with Iceman and Firestar.

Spider-Man: The Animated Series (1994-1998): A highly popular animated show that introduced many fans to Spider-Man's world and villains. This show is beloved for its faithful adaptations of the comic storylines.

The Spectacular Spider-Man (2008-2009): A highly praised animated series for its character development and storytelling.

Ultimate Spider-Man (2012-2017): Focused on a younger Spider-Man and integrated the character more into the larger Marvel Universe.

Spider-Man in Live-Action Adaptations

  • The Sam Raimi Trilogy (2002-2007)
  • The Amazing Spider-Man Series (2012-2014)
  • Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) Spider-Man (2016-Present)
  • Animated Spider-Man Movies

Summary

From his humble beginnings in the pages of comic books in 1962 to becoming a pop culture phenomenon across various forms of media, Spider-Man has remained a beloved and influential superhero. His journey through comics, animated series, live-action films, and video games reflects his adaptability and enduring appeal.
